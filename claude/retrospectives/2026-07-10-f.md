## Key decisions, and why

- **Name `focused-spikes`, not `active-`/`working-`/`new-`.** Status/lifecycle words impose a
  maintenance *duty* the folder cannot honor — spikes legitimately linger (e.g. as future
  pedagogical products), so a lifecycle name drifts out of truth and manufactures a false
  "why isn't this cleaned up?" signal. `focused` is an **intrinsic, time-invariant property**
  and pairs against the legacy set's "throwaway."
- **The recurring principle: prefer the intrinsic / move-invariant property over the positional
  one.** It drove *every* sub-decision — `focused` (a quality) over lifecycle for the name; a
  **marker file** (`status_active.md`) over a parent dir for status; **cite-by-name-through-the-
  index** over cite-by-path for identity; a **`shared/` trunk** over leaf-to-leaf imports for
  dependencies. That they all rhyme is why the design felt settled rather than arbitrary.
- **Self-containment = the mv-test** (move a spike's folder and it must still run). This makes
  internal org a free filing decision *and* makes a spike portable (the pedagogy endgame). Two
  senses of "standalone" were teased apart: **relocation-safe** (may `import pypiano_2607`; the
  active baseline) vs **fully self-contained / extractable** (must vendor; the archived tier).
- **active→archived is a *hardening* step, not mere filing.** Archiving inlines external deps
  (`shared/`, the library) so the spike becomes extractable — re-earning the legacy freeze's
  stability per-spike, by choice ("best-effort immutability").
- **Status via an in-folder marker, not a parent dir** — chosen because it *dissolves* the
  cross-reference fragility: a status change is an edit-in-place, never a move, so a spike's
  path stays stable for its whole life and inbound citations never break.
- **Tasks: prune (Option A) + a collapsed `done.md`.** The insight was "collapse on done" —
  full detail is needed while a task is active and collapses to a line once done. `done.md`
  earns its place as a terse, cross-cutting completion index at a distinct altitude from
  plan / retro / memory (not a 5th redundant copy).
- **The same collapse applied to the plan.** A completed plan collapses to as-built + durable
  decisions and is *kept as a record* (per the plans README), with process prose delegated to
  git + retros. Verified first that every durable claim was already in memory — nothing needed
  rescuing.
