## A reckoning: the computer-keyboard hardware ceiling

Playing the finished app on real hardware surfaced a **hard physical constraint** that any
future input design must reconcile with. It is a property of today's commodity computer
keyboards, not of this code:

- Simultaneous-key registration is limited and **uneven**. Some key combinations register
  as many as **six keys at once** — but there also exist **specific three-key combinations**
  that, once all three are held down, prevent any additional key from registering at all.
- This is a keyboard-matrix limitation (rollover / jamming that depends on the exact set of
  keys and varies from keyboard to keyboard), so it cannot be papered over in software.

The consequence is real: *the computer keyboard* ***cannot*** be treated as a general polyphonic
or chordal input surface. Which notes can sound together depends on the physical wiring of
the particular keyboard, and some combinations are simply unreachable from the keys. This
**severely constrains** what the instrument can express through the QWERTY path, and
overcoming it would demand **extraordinary UX innovation.** This retrospective only *records*
the reckoning — it offers no remedy.

## Milestone, and an open future

The project stands at a **milestone**: a complete, tested, human-played instrument with two
tunings, built cleanly from frozen spikes. **What happens next is an open question, and the
human's to decide.** Possibilities remain — the standing scope, for one, keeps any microtonal
work beyond JI as *future spikes* — but nothing here selects or urges a path. The instrument
works; where it goes is left deliberately open.

## What went well

- The **one-seam architecture** (router = pitch→Hz; synth device-free) let both the app and
  Just Intonation land as small, additive changes with the synth untouched.
- **Frozen-spike discipline + two test nets** held across all four increments.
- **Adversarial verification** — and hand-checking a workflow agent that errored rather than
  trusting a green it never produced — caught a real test gap and confirmed the JI math.
