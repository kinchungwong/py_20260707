## Follow-up: shell-safety of `--tonic` (same session)

The human flagged that `#` is the shell's comment character. **Empirically verified**:
`--tonic F#` works **unquoted** (bash passes `['--tonic','F#']`) because `#` only opens a
comment at a *word boundary* ‚Äî never in `--tonic X#`. But it's fragile in adjacent
contexts (a Makefile recipe comments `#` mid-line). This isn't a safety-vs-faithfulness
dichotomy: **enharmonic flats already gave every sharp a `#`-free spelling** (`Gb`==`F#`),
and `tonic_to_midi` accepted them. Per the human's call, also added an **`s`-suffix sharp**
(`Fs`==`F#`) so sharp-thinkers get a shell-safe spelling too; `#` stays accepted (faithful).
The `--tonic` help + examples now lead with the safe spellings. `Fs`/`F#`/`Gb` tune
identically (test-pinned). Takeaway: keep the faithful spelling *accepted*, but *advertise*
the shell-safe one so copy-paste is robust everywhere.
- **`InputRouter`** gained a **`tuning` parameter** (`__init__(tuning=midi_to_freq)`);
  `press()` now mints `freq = self._tuning(midi)`. **Backward-compatible** ‚Äî `InputRouter()`
  is byte-identical 12-TET. This is the pitch‚ÜíHz extension point the architecture always
  anticipated ("a cents/JI slot can live here later") finally used.
- **`PianoApp(voice=..., tuning=None)`** threads the tuning to the router and **preserves

- **`examples/play_app.py`** ‚Äî `--tuning 12tet|ji` (default 12tet) + `--tonic C` (note

## Decisions & why (canonical facts in `../plans/`)

- **Scope: Just Intonation ONLY** (human decision). Non-12-EDO / isomorphic layouts / JI
  from arbitrary frequency ratios are explicitly OUT of this library increment ‚Äî they need
  co-designed UI + mechanics and must begin as **new spikes**. Kept the diff tightly JI.
- **5-limit standard ratios + selectable tonic** (`--tonic`, default C), both human-chosen
  from a preview. Tonic anchored to 12-TET (the tonic matches ET; other notes just-tuned
  around it). No runtime toggle ‚Äî tuning is a launch-time choice (router/synth are
  construction-time), matching the "command-line option" ask.
- **The router is the single pitch‚ÜíHz seam**, so JI needed exactly one hook there; the
  synth, `sounder_id` identity, reconciliation, and voice allocation are all unchanged.
- **Spikes untouched** (explicit human instruction); `git diff claude/spikes` empty, spike
  net 14/14. Increment-4 edits are additive to router/app + the two `__init__`/test files.

## How it was built

Implemented directly (small, coherent diff), full suite green, then a **build ‚Üí test ‚Üí
adversarial-verify** pass. The verify workflow's *invariants* reviewer found nothing; its
*math* reviewer **errored** (agent failure, not a finding) ‚Äî so rather than trust a green
that wasn't actually produced, the JI math was **re-verified by hand** with a direct
computation (5-limit table + ET-deviation cents, anchoring, pure octaves, a non-C tonic
`A` case, tonic-octave cancellation across all MIDI, keyboard-range monotonicity,
name parsing, and the 12-TET-default backward-compat). All correct.
